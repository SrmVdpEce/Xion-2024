Path finder

About Event

Building a path-following robot involves understanding its fundamental concepts, selecting appropriate components, designing and assembling the robot, programming it to interpret sensor data and control its movement, and testing its performance under various conditions. Key considerations include the choice of platform, integration of sensors and actuators, and implementation of path-following algorithms such as PID control. Prototyping and programming are crucial stages, requiring meticulous calibration and testing to ensure accuracy and reliability. Refinement and optimization efforts focus on improving speed, stability, and energy efficiency. Looking ahead, the potential for scaling up and customizing the robot for specific applications is vast, with real-world applications ranging from logistics to agriculture and beyond, underscoring the importance of continued research and development in the field of robotics and automation.

Robo War

About Event

In the adrenaline-fueled arena of robot warfare, competitors pit their mechanical creations against one another in intense battles of strategy, skill, and engineering prowess. The content for a robot war event typically encompasses various components, including rules and regulations, competition format, and safety protocols. Organizers establish guidelines regarding permissible robot designs, weaponry, and operating parameters to ensure fairness and participant safety. The competition format may consist of one-on-one duels, team-based skirmishes, or even free-for-all brawls, each demanding different tactics and strategies from the participants. Spectators are treated to thrilling displays of innovation and creativity as robots equipped with an array of weapons, from spinning blades to pneumatic flippers, engage in fierce combat, aiming to immobilize or outmaneuver their opponents.
